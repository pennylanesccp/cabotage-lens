# Batch 001 External References

Access date for this research pass: 2026-06-25.

## 1. Purpose

This document collects external references needed to move Batch 001 validation cases beyond `reference_needed`.

The reference baseline is `docs/validation/tf_validation_batch_001_results.md`, and the reference needs are mapped in `docs/validation/tf_validation_batch_001_reference_needs.md`. The Batch 001 model outputs are preserved here exactly as documented there. No new model execution was performed for this research pass.

These references are used for plausibility and boundary comparison, not for perfect reconstruction of real shipments. In particular, the first-pass evidence is intended to identify whether the road, port-selection, maritime-distance, service, cost, and emissions boundaries are plausible enough for a later validation-status update.

## 2. Source quality rules

Source quality levels:

- High: official or regulatory source, port authority, ANTAQ, government database, primary shipping line schedule or service map, peer-reviewed literature, official MRV/IMO/IPCC/EMEP-style source.
- Medium: technical report, industry report, reputable logistics publication, company page with clear operational information.
- Low: generic calculator, unsourced blog, forum, AI-generated page, unclear commercial page.

Rules used in this pass:

- Prefer high-quality sources.
- Do not use low-quality sources as primary evidence unless no better source exists, and label them as low confidence.
- Record access date.
- Preserve units exactly as found.
- Convert units only in a clearly marked "derived comparison note"; do not silently convert.
- If a reference cannot be found, write `not found in this pass`.
- Do not invent values.

## 3. Batch-level reference findings

Maritime/corridor references were found for several relevant Brazilian cabotage nodes through a peer-reviewed 2025 Journal of Transport Geography paper by Costa et al., hosted in the USP repository. The paper states that its cabotage port distances are in nautical miles and that Appendix 6 is based on ANTAQ data. Its table uses dots as thousands separators, so `3.300` is read as `3300` nautical miles. This source covers `BRSSZ` Santos, `BRMAO` Manaus, `BRPEC` Pecem, `BRSUA` Suape, `BRSSA` Salvador, and `BRRIG` Rio Grande, but it does not cover Porto de Fortaleza, Porto do Recife, or Porto de Angra dos Reis as separate nodes.

Service-plausibility references were found for Santos/Manaus, Manaus/Pecem or Manaus/Fortaleza-region service, Rio Grande cabotage service, Suape/Pernambuco cabotage service, and Fortaleza cabotage activity. Strong evidence was not found for Angra dos Reis as a container/cabotage origin. On the contrary, PortosRio states that Angra dos Reis has no container movement.

Road-distance references were collected from public OSRM/OpenStreetMap route API calls using city-centre coordinates obtained through OpenStreetMap/Nominatim in this pass. These are useful independent routing checks, but they remain map-provider references, not official freight-route audits. They are especially weak for Amazon/Northern routes where road, ferry, river, seasonality, and graph assumptions may differ materially.

Emissions references were collected at boundary level. The Log-In CO2 calculator page states that its calculations follow the Brazilian GHG Protocol Program methodology specifications and IPCC guidelines. The Costa et al. cabotage paper records marine fuel energy/emissions factors in `gCO2eq/MJ` and fuel-cost inputs. These references are not a full validation of Batch 001 emissions outputs because they do not reproduce the model's truck class, vessel allocation, port operations, or TTW/WTW/LCA boundary.

Cost-boundary references were collected at boundary level. The ANP fuel-price page provides official weekly fuel-price survey files for diesel lookup in Brazil. The Costa et al. cabotage paper records MDO and VLSFO cost inputs from Shipandbunker (2024) in `USD/ton`. These should not be compared directly with Batch 001 full road/multimodal costs without date, currency, fuel-only versus full-cost, port-cost, toll, labor, margin, and handling-boundary checks.

Do not classify the cases as pass/fail in `tf_validation_batch_001_results.md` from this document alone. Evidence has been collected; status updates should be handled in a later issue. The only case with strong operational-plausibility evidence against the selected model port is `TF-VAL-004`, because the model selected Angra dos Reis as a container/cabotage origin even though the port authority states there is no container movement there.
