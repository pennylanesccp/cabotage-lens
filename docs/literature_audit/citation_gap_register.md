# Citation Gap Register

This document identifies claims or methodology components within the CabotageLens project that currently lack sufficiently strong sources from the local literature audit.

| Component / Claim | Current Source | Why Support is Insufficient | Suggested Type of Source Needed | Priority | Proposed Next Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Specific truck emissions factors in Brazil** | Generic assumptions / local data | Papers reviewed focus heavily on the maritime side or European road transport (`marmara2021`, `shortsea2019`). | A recent Brazilian study or agency report (e.g., ANTT, EPE) detailing average fleet age and emissions factors for heavy-duty trucks in Brazil. | High | Search for Brazilian road emissions baselines to complement the maritime focus. |
| **Cabotage vessel capacity utilization averages** | Assumed constants | Competitiveness heavily depends on utilization, but specific average load factors for Brazilian cabotage aren't explicitly detailed in the reviewed abstracts. | ANTAQ statistical reports or studies specifically measuring slot utilization on the Brazilian coast. | High | Extract utilization data from `icct2022` if present, otherwise locate ANTAQ yearly reports. |
| **Port dwell times / Terminal efficiency in Brazil** | `berth2009`, `shipops2022` | Current sources for port ops are older (2009) or regional (SE Asia). | Studies on Brazilian port efficiency (e.g., Santos, Suape) detailing average time-at-berth and crane moves per hour. | Medium | Search for ANTAQ port performance statistics or local case studies. |
| **Commercial freight rate margins** | None | The model estimates operational costs but warns against equating them to freight rates. However, there is no source defining the typical margin. | Logistics industry reports or economic papers on Brazilian freight margins. | Low | Decide if defining the margin is necessary; if not, maintain the strong limitation statement. |
| **Definition of the theoretical boundary (TTW)** | `maritimelca2024` (provides WTW/LCA context) | While `maritimelca2024` defines LCA, a definitive source stating that TTW is a standard acceptable boundary for operational comparison would strengthen the defense. | Guidelines from IMO, EPA, or a methodological standard for transport comparisons. | Medium | Cite established maritime or transport frameworks (e.g., GLEC framework) for bounding definitions. |
